About WeatherPal

While planning a move from Florida to Boulder, CO, I ran into a snag - people said the weather in Colorado was "schizophrenic", "weird", or "unpredictable", but outside of these vague generalizations they couldn't say more. "You'll have to experience it for yourself."

What? And go outside?

I found NOAA's QCLCD dataset, got to work, and quickly found myself looking at a simple line graph of a years' worth of hourly temperature readings from Denver International Airport. And that's how WeatherPal got its start.

WeatherPal is still a work in progress.
International weather stations, sadly, aren't yet included. The station comparison could use a bit more of a human touch. The prevailing wind calculation is totally wrong. But WeatherPal is probably good enough to be a useful resource for some folks, so it's online. I hope you find it useful.
